Problem Solving

23 September 2022


The problem I encountered

The problem I was facing was Buzz buzz buZZ.
What I needed to do was get rid of the word “buzz” regardless of capitalization in the sentence [Buzz BUzz BuZZ help BUZZ buzz]. I wanted to use the filter method to filter out all the buzzes so only the word “help” will stay. But I got blocked by what to filter. For example, if you want to only look up meat when you do online grocery shopping, you put “Meat & Seafood” in the filter. And you will only see products that meet the condition you put in which are meat and seafood on your web page. I understood the result I wanted to get and the method I needed to use, but I was lost in finding the connection between them.

The problem-solving techniques I used were reading error message and asking for help. The aha moment was when I read the error message that told me what was expected and what was the result of my code. It let me see the outcome was “buzz buzz buzz buzz buzz”. So I got it immediately that I was doing the opposite thing. I put “buzz” in the filter, so only “buzz” stayed. I changed the condition in the filter that was everything that doesn't equal “buzz”.

I felt a bit defeated and anxious throughout the process. I was lost in the logic flow and didn't see the whole picture. What I learned here is to do it step by step, and find out what went wrong. Knowing the method used to solve the problem is not everything. I also need to know working theory and use reverse thinking. The feedback is essential as well. Just like the doctor can only help their patients when knowing what kind of disease they are tackling.

Elegantly solved a problem

The problem was "We built this city". At first I had trouble undestandinging the requirements and wasn't sure what to do.
I tried Pseudocode and Reading test messages to figure out what I needed to achieve. And what should be included and the types of certain elements. I also tried Console.logging to see the results and what is lacking, and what went wrong. From that, I used Googling to find methods to solve the problem.

I felt confident throughout the process as I know exactly what to do step by step. And I can see what went wrong if it wasn't working.
I learned it is important to break big things down into small pieces and solve them step by step in a logical way. Also, feedbacks such as reading error messages helps to locate the problem, saving time and effort to go through things all over again. Last but not least, searching skills are crucial in problem-solving, especially with the error messages we get.

Reflect on how confident you feel using the problem-solving techniques and process:

Pseudocode
Pseudocode is an informal language that programmers use to create an outline or a rough draft of a program.
I used it to be clear about what needs to be tackled and the methods and steps that will lead me to it. I tend to work things out in my head or write them down somewhere by hand. And I need more practise on Pseudocode.

Trying something
Problem-solving is about trying something out. I constantly try different methods and ways to get the code working. It is a effective way for me to practice and know what works and what doesn't. And most importantly why. I feel confident using this method.

Rubber ducky method The method is referred to as a think-aloud protocol. Programmers explain the problem out loud to get your brain into problem-solving mode. When they talk to a rubber duck, they can get a better idea of their thought processes and gain a deeper understanding of their work.
For me to be qualified to use the method I need a Rubber ducky XD. But speaking to myself is more affordable. I walk through the requirement with myself or sometimes with my partner. And then ask myself in order to achieve that, what methods or functions do I need? Then I will try out the ways till I get a pass message. Rubber ducky method is useful for me and I will practise more.

Reading error messages
The error messages are helpful to spot the problem and show a direction into solving it.
I used this method a lots and found it very helpful in terms of what and where went wrong. It helps googling as I now have the key words which narrow down the range. I need to do more study to undertand about the error types.

Console.logging
Console.logging allows programmers to read the code on a web page and therefore help to test the written code.
I love Console.logging as it shows the results of the code. And it gives me idea what is the different between the actual results and what is expected. I think I am becoming better in it.

Googling
You can almost google everything, and get millions of answers. It is important that you know which one will suit the problem you encounter the most.
It is the most important method I reckon as I get help from people around the world and there is often a solution that works for the error that occured. Although I need to learn more about searching skills.

Asking your peers for help
It is always nice to get support from people who work on the same project as you. However, I have not had chance to as the problems I encounted are solved by previous methods. I will try this method in a near further.

Asking coaches for help
I did not use this method enough. I guess it can be hard sometimes to ask for help from the coaches as I do not want them to think I am asking silly questions. But through learning growth mindset, I came to realise it is a normal process that we feel inconpetent when strating new things. And I will do it more often.

Improving your process with reflection
I often ask myself whether I can still solve the same problem without the answer and what are other methods I can use to improve my problem-solving skills. The most important thing is to make sense of the process and apply it to the problems you encounter.
