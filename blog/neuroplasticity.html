Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

23 September 2022


What is Neuroplasticity

The term "neuroplasticity" (or "brain plasticity") refers to the ability of our brains to reorganize, both physically and functionally, throughout our lives, due to our environment. In short, it refers to the brain's ability to change itself.

Some principles of Neuroplasticity and Why it is important

Use It or Lose It: failure to drive specific brain functions can lead to loss of abilities.

Use It and Improve It: training that drives a specific brain function can lead to an enhancement of that function.

Repetition: repetition of a learned (or re-learned) behavior is required for long-term changes in the brain. You need thousands of repetitions to master a skill. The more time you spend practicing, the better you perform.

Intensity: induction of plasticity requires sufficient training intensity.

Salience: emotions can affect the strength of memory consolidation.

Transference: training one specific skill can sometimes activate the pathways of nearby, untrained items and improve those skills as well.

Understanding the principles allowing us to be confident about improving the brain's ability into engaging and acquiring know knowledge and experience. It challenges the limits we set for ourselves or by society. To use the brain and challenge yourself frequently, you will find out how powerful your brain is, and there are so many things we can achieve that we did not dare to believe.

Knowing the principle of neuroplasticity helps me understand our brain can be "shaped" and "sharpened". It also give me confidence to believe in myself. As I now know it takes time, practise, and repetition to gain a skill set. Constantly learning can help my brain become more adaptable and capable.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Constantly learning and absorbing new information and knowledge. Always use my brain and expose myself to an information-rich environment. The stimulation of all kinds of activities can strengthen neural connections over time.

Repeate and stick to it. When I just started learning Japanese, the word “sunflower” was so difficult for me. So I repeated it around a hundred times. And now I can never forget it even I stopped learning the language for 5 years. Practicing frequently and consistently is the key.

What is Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is that individuals who believe their talents can be developed (through hard work, good strategies, and input from others).

People with a growth mindset are more likely to achieve more than those with a more fixed mindset (those who believe their talents are innate gifts). A “growth mindset” thrives on challenge and sees failure not as evidence of unintelligence but as a heartening springboard for growth and stretching our existing abilities.

We need to understand this because sometimes we might experience Imposter Syndrome (it is defined as doubting your abilities and feeling like a fraud) even when we are actually doing alright. Having a hard time understanding or failing a couple of times can be scary. But that does not define us and definitely does not mean we can't do it ever. Accepting the fact that the knowledge is new to us and may be challenging, having faith in ourselves, and knowing how much more we will learn and grow in this process can help us ease the nerves and build the confidence to achieve the goal.

Inspiration of Growth Mindset

In order to do everything perfectly and not make any mistakes, I sometimes avoid doing it from the beginning. But this exploration has me realise that failure is not the end. It is just another challenge and a great moment to reflect on for further exploration and development. It definitely changed my mindset. And made me become braver and able to embrace failure and imperfection.
